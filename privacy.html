Privacy Policy

At HomieHoney, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This policy outlines how we collect, use, and safeguard your data.

Introduction

Welcome to HomieHoney's Privacy Policy. This document explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services. Please read this policy carefully. By accessing or using our service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information from and about users of our website, including personal identification information (name, email address, phone number), demographic information, payment details, technical data about your device, and usage data about how you interact with our website. This information helps us provide better services and improve user experience.

2. How We Collect Information

We collect information directly when you register, place an order, subscribe to our newsletter, or fill out a form. We also collect data automatically through cookies and similar technologies as you navigate our site. Additionally, we may receive information from third parties like payment processors or social media platforms when you interact with us through these services.

3. Use of Your Information

We use collected information to process transactions, provide customer support, personalize your experience, improve our website, send periodic emails, administer contests or promotions, and for security and fraud prevention. Your information helps us respond more effectively to your service requests and support needs.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on several legal grounds: your consent, the necessity to fulfill our contractual obligations to you, compliance with legal requirements, and our legitimate business interests (like improving our services and preventing fraud). You may withdraw consent at any time where applicable.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. When determining retention periods, we consider the amount, nature, and sensitivity of the data, potential risk from unauthorized use, and legal requirements.

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These include encryption, access controls, secure servers, and regular security assessments. However, no internet transmission is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and maintained on computers located outside your jurisdiction where data protection laws may differ. For transfers outside the European Economic Area, we implement safeguards like standard contractual clauses to ensure your data remains protected according to EU standards.

8. Your Data Protection Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights including access to your data, correction of inaccurate information, deletion of data, restriction of processing, data portability, objection to processing, and withdrawal of consent. You also have the right to lodge complaints with a supervisory authority.

9. Third-Party Disclosures

We may share information with trusted third parties who assist us in operating our website, conducting business, or servicing you, so long as they agree to keep it confidential. We may also release information when legally required or to protect rights, property, or safety. We never sell your personal data to third parties.

10. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance user experience, analyze trends, administer the website, track movements around the site, and gather demographic information. You can control cookies through your browser settings, but disabling them may affect functionality.

11. Do Not Track Signals

Our website does not currently respond to Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals as no uniform standard for DNT has been adopted. We continue to monitor developments around DNT browser technology and any emerging standards.

12. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13 (or 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we discover we've collected such data, we will delete it immediately. Parents can contact us to review or delete any data about their child.

13. California Privacy Rights

California residents have specific rights under the CCPA, including knowing what personal data is collected, accessing their data, requesting deletion, and opting out of data sales. We do not sell personal information. California users may request information about disclosures for direct marketing purposes.

14. GDPR Compliance

For EU users, we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This includes providing transparent information about data processing, ensuring lawful bases for processing, implementing data protection measures, and respecting user rights regarding their personal data.

15. Payment Information

All payment transactions are processed through secure gateways and are not stored on our servers. We use PCI-compliant third-party payment processors who adhere to strict security standards. We only receive information necessary to fulfill your order (like billing address).

16. Newsletter and Marketing

When you subscribe to our newsletter, we collect your email address to send updates and marketing communications. You can unsubscribe anytime using the link in each email. We may analyze open and click rates to improve our communications, but this data is aggregated and anonymized.

17. User Accounts

Account registration requires personal information to provide services. You can access, update, or delete your account information through your profile settings. We encourage strong passwords and two-factor authentication for account security.

18. Public Forums and Reviews

Information you post in public areas (like reviews or forums) is publicly available. Be cautious about sharing personal details. We cannot control how others may use this information. You can request removal of your content by contacting us.

19. Affiliate Links

Some links may be affiliate links that earn us commissions. These don't affect your price or our recommendations. Clicking these links may place cookies on your device to track referrals, as explained in our Cookie Policy.

20. Social Media Features

Our website includes social media features that may collect your IP address and set cookies. These features are hosted by third parties, and your interactions are governed by their privacy policies. We encourage you to review these policies before engaging.

21. Testimonials

With your consent, we may display personal testimonials on our site. If you wish to update or delete your testimonial, contact us. We may anonymize testimonials for use in marketing materials.

22. Surveys and Contests

Participation in surveys or contests is voluntary. Contact information collected will be used to notify winners and award prizes. Survey information helps improve our services and may be used for research purposes.

23. Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction. We will notify you of any such change and outline your choices regarding your information.

24. Data Breach Procedures

We have procedures to identify, assess, and respond to data breaches. In case of a breach that risks your rights, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by law, typically within 72 hours of discovery.

25. Third-Party Websites

Our site contains links to other websites. We're not responsible for their content or privacy practices. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

26. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy periodically. We'll notify you of significant changes through email or a notice on our website. Your continued use after changes constitutes acceptance. We encourage regular review of this policy.

27. Data Protection Officer

We have appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee compliance with data protection laws. You can contact our DPO with any questions or concerns about your personal data and our privacy practices.

28. Automated Decision Making

We do not use fully automated decision-making processes that significantly affect users. Any automated processing (like fraud detection) includes human review before final decisions are made that could impact users.

29. Data Minimization

We only collect data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes for which it is processed. We periodically review our data collection practices to ensure compliance with this principle.

30. Accuracy of Data

We take reasonable steps to ensure personal data is accurate and kept up to date. Users can update their information through account settings or by contacting us. We implement processes to identify and correct inaccurate data.

31. Storage Limitations

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes collected, including legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. We have established retention policies and schedules for different categories of data.

32. Integrity and Confidentiality

We process personal data in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against unauthorized or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. We use technical and organizational measures to achieve this.

33. Accountability

We are responsible for demonstrating compliance with data protection principles. This includes maintaining documentation of processing activities, implementing appropriate policies, and conducting regular audits of our data practices.

34. Data Protection by Design

We implement data protection principles at the design stage of any new system or process involving personal data. This includes minimizing data collection, limiting access, and implementing security features from the outset.

35. Data Protection Impact Assessments

For high-risk processing activities, we conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments to identify and mitigate risks to data subjects. These assessments are reviewed regularly and when processing changes occur.

36. Vendor Management

We carefully select third-party vendors who process personal data on our behalf. Contracts with these processors include strict data protection obligations, and we conduct audits to ensure compliance with these requirements.

37. Employee Training

We provide regular data protection training to employees who handle personal data. This training covers legal requirements, company policies, and security best practices to ensure proper handling of personal information.

38. Record Keeping

We maintain records of processing activities as required by law, including purposes of processing, data categories, recipients, retention periods, and security measures. These records are available to supervisory authorities upon request.

39. Subject Access Requests

We have established procedures to handle requests from individuals exercising their data protection rights. These requests are processed within one month, with possible extension for complex requests. No fee is charged unless the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

40. Privacy by Default

We implement privacy-friendly default settings that automatically protect personal data. Users must take positive action to share data more widely. Our systems are designed to collect only necessary data by default.

Effective Date: This policy was last updated on July 1, 2023.
